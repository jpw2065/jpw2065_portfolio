Audio Visualizer

(2020 October)

This is the project that I am most proud of at the current moment. The reason being is the complexity this project took to undertake and the speed at which I was able to handle such complexity (about one week). This project is two-fold, the first being an audio visualizer, and the second being computer vision gesture controls. The design of the audio visualizer was crafted to give an elegant look to the interface. By allowing the user to hide elements of the user interface it created a clean look to the website. The actual audio visualizer portion was programmed using Javascript, HTML, and CSS. I then used a Tensorflow trained model to track the hands of the user by performing a linear regression on a series of points. Based on the slope of these points I was able to determine which direction the user swiped their hand. I ended up training my model with the EgoHands dataset and utilizing the Tensorflow python library. Overall, this was my first time doing machine learning, and I learned a whole host of new concepts and topics. I always love going into a new area when doing a project, and this project is a prime example of that. Eventually, I plan on making the hand tracking more efficient with improved data set and more efficient models. I am fascinated by different ways of interacting with a computer, and I see great potential in gesture controls once the field of machine learning expands!

Evolution Simulator

(2020 Septemmber)

The goal I had in this project was to give a user the ability to interact with a simulation to change its outcome. I landed on creating an evolution simulator that gives the user controls to affect the creatures that are evolved. It works through the process of Q-Learning, basically giving these creatures reinforcement in terms of gaining the most amount of energy as possible, which acts as a way to maximize the creature's reward. The outcomes can be quite interesting. Give it a try!

CREDIT

(2020 - Current)

CREDIT otherwise known as Commercial Real Estate Deal Information Tracker is a web application that I currently work on for the client Largo Capital Inc. CREDIT is the method of choice for Largo to keep track of real estate deal information. Some highlights during my time at Largo included:

  • Working alongside the business to turn their business needs into technical solutions.
  • Creating a white label site to expand our application's customer base.
  • Participating in daily standup meetings in an agile workflow environment.
  • Creating a 6-month technical roadmap for the direction that Largo wants to take CREDIT in.
  • Modernized the website interface to optimize the user experience using Bootstrap 4, HTML, CSS, and TWIG.
  • Creating a permissions system that regulated which users had access to our application. Utilized RBAC permission controls.

The application was developed using Laravel (PHP) and is structured in an MVC framework. We use PostgreSQL relational database to store data in the backend. Overall developing CREDIT taught me the intricacies of developing a web application for a larger customer base. Most importantly, it taught me how to take business needs and finding a way to utilize technology to satisfy the needs of the client.

Pricetool

(2018 - 2020)

The Pricetool is a web application that I developed for my client YourOnlineMarketplace. The company needed a method for keeping track of inventory, and also the products we sold on our Amazon store. I was able to help develop a variety of different tools for the business to use, such as an inventory scanning system, data visualization tools, and also product tracking tools.
